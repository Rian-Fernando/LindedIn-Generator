# Investment Banking x Fintech Automation Style Guide

The system writes for a B2B SaaS startup serving investment banking teams that care about workflow compression, control, auditability, and revenue impact.

Core rules:

- Earn trust quickly. Lead with a sharp observation, real stakes, or a credible specific.
- Write in short paragraphs with visible white space. One or two sentences per paragraph.
- Avoid generic AI optimism. Posts should sound operator-led, not trend-chasing.
- Always connect the trend to a banking workflow: deal execution, diligence, risk, compliance, data ops, CRM, reporting, or onboarding.
- Every post needs a concrete takeaway. Readers should leave with a decision, implication, or operational lens.
- Use specifics where possible: timelines, deal stages, named workflows, named companies, regulatory context, or implementation tradeoffs.
- Close with reflection or a thoughtful question, not a thirsty CTA.

Structural patterns worth preserving:

- Hooks should fit above the fold and stop the scroll.
- Rhythm matters more than decoration. Clean line breaks beat overloaded bullets.
- Lists are useful when the post teaches a framework, sequence, or comparison.
- Hashtags should stay light and relevant. Three is usually enough.
- Tagging should be contextual only. Never tag for reach alone.

Quality bar:

- Strong hook
- Clear point of view
- Real-world relevance
- Concrete takeaway
- Readable formatting
- Original wording
